Brand history — 12 events (launched / acquired / divested)

  • 2009-onward divested · Ratnagiri Gas & Power (RGPPL) — Erstwhile Enron/Dabhol asset; costly/scarce gas left CCGT running far below capacity — Underperformed
  • 2010s divested · Stalled/cancelled greenfield thermal projects — Various coal projects delayed/shelved over land, fuel-linkage and environmental clearances — Discontinued
  • 2011-onward divested · Trincomalee / Sampur (Sri Lanka) project — Original 500 MW Sampur coal plant scrapped over environmental/political issues; recast as 50 MW solar — Recast/delayed
  • 2012-onward divested · Gas-based generation fleet — Domestic gas shortage left ~6 GW gas capacity heavily underutilized for years — Underperformed
  • 2016 division · GoI sold 5% via OFS (Feb-2016) plus employee offer at 5% dis — Part of CPSE disinvestment programme (Business Standard / DIPAM)
  • 2020 acquired · Acquired GoI stakes in THDC India and NEEPCO — Entered large-scale hydro; both became subsidiaries 27-Mar-2020 (NTPC official page)
  • 2020 subsidiary · Incorporated NTPC Renewable Energy Ltd (NREL) — Dedicated build-own-operate RE vehicle (NTPC official JV/Subs page)
  • 2022 acquired · Acquired Jhabua Power 600 MW via insolvency (CIRP) — First IBC-route plant acquisition (BSE filing / press)
  • 2022 subsidiary · Incorporated NTPC Green Energy Ltd (NGEL) as renewables hold — Consolidated green portfolio under one entity, 07-Apr-2022 (NTPC official page)
  • 2024 jv · NGEL formed RE JV with ONGC Green — NGEL-ONGC renewables JV announced Nov-2024 (Business Standard)
  • 2024 listed subsidiary · NTPC Green Energy IPO on NSE/BSE — Listed 27-Nov-2024; price band Rs 102-108 (BSE/NSE filings; NGEL prospectus)
  • 2025 associate · NPCIL-NTPC restructured ASHVINI nuclear JV — Amendment to scale civil nuclear build-out (World Nuclear News)
